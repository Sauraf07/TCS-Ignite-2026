# TCS Ignite Exam Preparation Guide

*Last updated: July 2026 — based on the most recent publicly available TCS/TCS iON information. Exam patterns are revised periodically by TCS, so always cross-check the latest details on your TCS NextStep dashboard once you register.*

---

## 1. Exam Overview

**TCS B.Sc Ignite (and Smart Hiring)** is TCS's dedicated recruitment drive for non-engineering science graduates — students from **BCA, B.Sc (IT, Computer Science, Maths, Data Science, Statistics, Physics, Chemistry, Electronics, Cybersecurity, Biochemistry)** and **B.Voc (CS/IT)** backgrounds. It exists alongside TCS's engineering-focused drives (TCS NQT, TCS Ninja, TCS Digital) to give science/BCA graduates a direct path into IT roles.

Key facts:
- **Two tracks, one test:** Candidates apply under **TCS Ignite** or **TCS Smart**. Both go through a **single integrated online test**; your performance decides which category (and eventual role/interview track) you're shortlisted for.
- **Ignite vs Smart — the key difference:** In TCS Ignite, the **coding/programming question is mandatory**. In TCS Smart, attempting the coding section is optional. This is the single biggest structural distinction to know.
- **Test provider:** TCS iON (the same platform used for TCS NQT).
- **Eligibility:** 3-year full-time graduates (2025/2026 batch) in the disciplines listed above; minimum 50% aggregate (or equivalent CGPA) across Class 10, Class 12, Diploma (if any), and Graduation, with no active backlogs at the time of appearing (2026 batch is allowed up to one active backlog, provided it's cleared before joining).
- **Career angle:** TCS Ignite is positioned as an accelerated-growth track, with TCS advertising potential compensation increases for high performers within 1–2 years.
- **Registration:** Done via the **TCS NextStep Portal** — sign up, verify Aadhaar details, select category "IT" (not "BPS"), fill the application, and click "Apply for Drive."
- **No exam fee** is charged at any stage; TCS is not affiliated with any paid agent for this process.

**Selection process (typical flow):**
1. Online Test (Numerical + Verbal + Reasoning + Coding)
2. Shortlisting via TCS NextStep Portal
3. Interview round(s) — technical/HR, format depends on the profile
4. Offer letter → document verification → joining letter

## 2. Exam Pattern

The TCS Smart Hiring/Ignite test runs on the TCS NQT platform, so its non-coding sections mirror the TCS NQT structure closely, with the coding section behaving differently (mandatory for Ignite, optional for Smart).

| Section | Questions | Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Numerical Ability | 20 Qs | 30 min | Quant/aptitude |
| Verbal Ability | 20 Qs | 30 min | English/grammar/RC |
| Reasoning Ability | 20 Qs | 30 min | Logical/analytical |
| Coding Ability | 1 question | 30 min | **Mandatory for Ignite**, optional for Smart |
| **Total** | **~61 Qs** | **~120 min** | |

**General rules that apply across sections:**
- **No negative marking.**
- **Non-adaptive test** — difficulty stays constant; it does not get harder/easier based on your performance.
- **Sequential/locked navigation** — once you move to the next question, you generally cannot go back (this is the pattern used in the broader TCS NQT-style tests; confirm on your specific admit card instructions).
- Test is conducted **online (remote)** and at **TCS iON authorized centres**, depending on your drive.
- Calculator and rough space are typically provided on-screen (no physical calculator/paper in remote-proctored formats).
- TCS periodically tweaks section timings and question counts between hiring cycles — **always verify the exact pattern shown in your official test instructions/dashboard before exam day**, since numbers above reflect the most recent publicly documented pattern and can shift by a few questions/minutes cycle to cycle.

**Coding round specifics (Ignite):**
- Typically 1 programming problem to be solved in ~30 minutes.
- Supported languages generally include C, C++, Java, and Python (verify allowed languages on your admit card).
- Focus is on correctness and logic more than optimization — solving the problem completely matters more than micro-optimizing.

- Sentence Completion / Word Completion

- Sentence Arrangement / Passage Ordering

- Grammar (formal/informal usage, sentence joining)
- Cloze Test (Fill in the Blanks)

### C. Reasoning Ability (Logical Reasoning)
- Letter Series & Word Patterns
- Number Series
- Coding–Decoding
- Blood Relations
- Seating Arrangements & Data Arrangements
- Directional Sense
- Syllogism
- Data Sufficiency
- Statement & Conclusion / Decision Making

- Basic programming logic: loops, conditionals, arrays, strings
- Data structures fundamentals (arrays, basic string manipulation)
- Problem-solving in C, C++, Java, or Python

- Input/output handling in your chosen language

- Core CS/IT fundamentals: DBMS basics, SQL, Operating Systems basics
- Final year academic project discussion
- HR round: communication, behavioral fit, relocation/flexibility

## 5. Learning Material & Strategy

### Priority topics (highest weight, focus here first)
1. **Numerical Ability:** Percentages, Profit & Loss, Time-Speed-Distance, Time & Work, Number Series — these appear in nearly every cycle.
2. **Reasoning:** Seating Arrangement, Blood Relations, Syllogism, Number/Letter Series — consistently high-frequency.
3. **Verbal:** Error Identification, Sentence Completion, Reading Comprehension — quick wins if grammar basics are solid.
4. **Coding (Ignite-critical):** Being able to write a complete, compiling, correct solution under time pressure matters more than knowing advanced algorithms. Practice simple-to-medium DSA problems (arrays, strings, basic loops/recursion) in your strongest language.

### Suggested 6-Week Study Plan

**Week 1–2: Foundations**
- Daily 1–1.5 hrs Numerical Ability (concept + 20 practice Qs/day)
- Daily 1 hr Reasoning (concept + 15 practice Qs/day)
- Daily 30 min Verbal (grammar rules + vocabulary)
- 3x/week: 1 coding problem (easy level) in your chosen language

**Week 3–4: Depth & Speed**
- Move to topic-wise timed practice sets (aim to match/beat section time limits)
- Start solving previous-year-style sectional tests
- Coding: 1 problem/day, alternating easy/medium; focus on writing clean, compiling code quickly
- Begin one full-length mock test per week

**Week 5: Full Mock Practice**
- 2–3 full-length mock tests (all sections, timed exactly like the real exam)
- Review every wrong answer — categorize mistakes as "concept gap," "silly error," or "time pressure"
- Revise weak topics identified from mocks

**Week 6: Final Revision**
- Daily light revision (formula sheets, error logs, coding syntax cheat sheet)
- 1–2 more full mocks, focusing on time management and accuracy under pressure
- Rest the day before the exam — avoid cramming new topics

*(If you have more or less time before your exam date, compress or extend proportionally, but always end with 1–2 weeks of full-length timed mocks.)*

### Section-wise Tips

- **Numerical Ability:** Speed comes from memorizing shortcuts (squares/cubes up to 30, common fraction-percentage conversions) — don't solve everything from first principles under time pressure.
- **Reasoning:** Practice puzzles (seating arrangement, blood relations) daily; these are time-consuming if unfamiliar but fast once you recognize the pattern.
- **Verbal:** Read a little English news/editorial daily to build RC speed; keep a running list of commonly tested synonyms/antonyms.
- **Coding:** Read the problem twice before coding. Since Ignite makes this mandatory, a partially correct but compiling solution is far better than an ambitious but broken one — prioritize getting a working baseline solution first, then optimize if time remains.

## 6. Practice & Mock Tests

**Why mocks matter more than passive reading:**
- They train **time management** — with ~30 minutes per section, pacing is often the real challenge, not knowledge.
- They surface **silly errors** (misreading questions, calculation slips) that pure concept study won't catch.
- They build **exam-day stamina** for sitting through a ~2-hour, no-going-back format.

**Recommendations:**
- Take at least **4–6 full-length mock tests** in your final two weeks, simulating exact section timings.
- After every mock, do a **structured review**: for each wrong answer, note whether it was a concept gap, a careless mistake, or a timing issue — this changes what you study next.
- Simulate the **no-negative-marking, sequential-navigation** format so the real exam's rules don't surprise you.
- If your TCS NextStep dashboard offers official TCS iON practice tests, prioritize those over third-party mocks — they'll most closely match real question style and interface.
