# 📚 Study Phases

## Phase 1 — Understand the Exam

Before studying any topic:

- Read the exam pattern
- Understand the syllabus
- Download the official sample paper
- Learn the sectional timings
- Know the question types

⏱️ Time Required: **1 Day**

---

## Phase 2 — Build Your Foundation

Focus only on concepts.

Don't worry about speed.

Understand:

- Formulas
- Logic
- Basic Programming
- Grammar Rules

---

## Phase 3 — Topic-wise Practice

Once concepts are clear,

Practice:

- Easy Questions
- Medium Questions
- Mixed Questions

Target:

✅ 50–100 questions per topic

---

## Phase 4 — Previous Year Questions

Now start solving:

- Memory-Based Questions
- Sample Papers
- TCS-style Questions

Observe:

- Repeated Topics
- Frequently Asked Patterns
- Time Taken

---

## Phase 5 — Mock Tests

Take full-length tests under real exam conditions.

Rules:

- No mobile phone
- No internet search
- Fixed timer
- Attempt all sections

After every mock:

- Review mistakes
- Note weak topics
- Revise formulas

---

## Phase 6 — Final Revision

Revise only:

- Notes
- Formula Sheets
- Important Tricks
- Frequently Asked Questions

Avoid learning new topics in the last few days.

# 💡 Pro Tips

> 📌 Consistency beats studying for long hours once in a while.

> 📌 Focus on understanding concepts before chasing speed.

> 📌 Analyze every mock test—learning from mistakes is as important as taking the test.

> 📌 If you choose to attempt the optional coding section, practice in one language consistently instead of switching between multiple languages.
