# ⏱️ Time Management Strategy

> **"Knowing the answers is not enough. Managing your time effectively is what helps you clear the TCS Ignite Assessment."**

---

## 📌 Overview

Time management is one of the most important skills during the TCS Ignite online assessment. Many candidates know the concepts but fail to complete the exam because they spend too much time on difficult questions.

This guide will help you:

- Understand how to divide your time efficiently.
- Learn when to skip questions.
- Improve your accuracy.
- Maximize your overall score.
- Avoid common mistakes made during the test.

---

# 📊 Exam Time Distribution

The TCS Ignite assessment consists of multiple sections with a total duration of **120 minutes**. While the exact interface and navigation rules may vary between hiring cycles, you should practice completing each section within its recommended time. Always follow the instructions shown in your actual assessment. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

| Section | Recommended Time |
|----------|-----------------:|
| Numerical Ability | 30 Minutes |
| Verbal Ability | 30 Minutes |
| Reasoning Ability | 30 Minutes |
| Coding | 30 Minutes |
| **Total** | **120 Minutes** |

---

# 🎯 Golden Rule

> **Don't spend too much time on a single question.**

If a question is taking longer than expected:

- Mark it (if the platform allows)
- Move on
- Return later if time permits

Remember:

> **Every question carries an opportunity. One difficult question should not cost you several easier ones.**

---

# 📈 Recommended Strategy

## Step 1 – Easy Questions

Spend the first few minutes solving questions that you find easy.

Benefits:

- Builds confidence
- Increases score quickly
- Reduces exam stress

---

## Step 2 – Medium Questions

Attempt questions that require calculation or deeper thinking.

Examples:

- Profit & Loss
- Time & Work
- Reading Comprehension
- Coding-Decoding

---

## Step 3 – Difficult Questions

Attempt these only after completing the easier ones.

Examples:

- Long Data Interpretation
- Complex Seating Arrangements
- Challenging Coding Problems

---

# ⏳ Suggested Time Per Question

## Numerical Ability

| Difficulty | Time |
|------------|------|
| Easy | 30–45 sec |
| Medium | 1–2 min |
| Difficult | 2–3 min (maximum) |

If you are stuck beyond this, move to the next question.

---

## Verbal Ability

| Question Type | Time |
|---------------|------|
| Vocabulary | 20–30 sec |
| Grammar | 30–45 sec |
| Reading Comprehension | 5–7 min per passage |

Read the questions first before reading the passage to save time.

---

## Reasoning Ability

| Question Type | Time |
|---------------|------|
| Series | 30 sec |
| Blood Relation | 1 min |
| Coding-Decoding | 1 min |
| Puzzle / Seating Arrangement | 4–6 min |

Do not spend too much time on a single puzzle.

---

## Coding

Suggested workflow:

- Understand the problem – 5 minutes
- Plan the solution – 5 minutes
- Write the code – 10–12 minutes
- Test with sample cases – 5–7 minutes
- Review and optimize – Remaining time

Avoid rewriting your entire solution unless absolutely necessary.

---

# 📋 Section-wise Strategy

## 🔢 Numerical Ability

### First 10 Minutes

Solve:

- Percentage
- Ratio
- Average
- Number System

These questions are often quicker to solve.

---

### Next 10 Minutes

Attempt:

- Time & Work
- Profit & Loss
- Speed, Time & Distance

---

### Final 10 Minutes

Attempt:

- Data Interpretation
- Probability
- Permutation & Combination

These may require more calculations.

---

## 🧠 Reasoning Ability

Recommended order:

1. Number Series
2. Coding-Decoding
3. Blood Relations
4. Direction Sense
5. Alphabet Series
6. Syllogism
7. Statement & Conclusion
8. Seating Arrangement
9. Puzzles

Start with high-confidence questions before tackling lengthy puzzles.

---

## 📖 Verbal Ability

Recommended order:

1. Vocabulary
2. Grammar
3. Fill in the Blanks
4. Error Detection
5. Sentence Rearrangement
6. Reading Comprehension

Reading Comprehension is usually the most time-consuming part.

---

## 💻 Coding

Recommended order:

- Read the entire problem carefully.
- Understand the input and output format.
- Solve sample test cases mentally.
- Handle edge cases.
- Write clean, readable code.
- Test before submitting.

---

# 🚦 When Should You Skip a Question?

Skip immediately if:

- You don't understand the question after reading it twice.
- The calculation is becoming excessively long.
- You're guessing multiple steps.
- You've already spent more time than planned.

Remember:

> **Skipping is a strategy, not a failure.**

---

# 📝 Accuracy vs Speed

Many candidates focus only on speed.

Instead, aim for:

- ✔ High accuracy
- ✔ Good speed
- ✔ Smart question selection

Accuracy generally contributes more than attempting every question.

---

# ⚡ Time-Saving Tips

### Numerical Ability

- Learn multiplication tables up to 25.
- Memorize squares up to 50.
- Memorize cubes up to 20.
- Learn percentage-to-fraction conversions.
- Practice mental arithmetic.

---

### Reasoning

- Avoid drawing unnecessary diagrams.
- Learn common patterns.
- Practice puzzles regularly.

---

### Verbal

- Read English articles daily.
- Improve vocabulary.
- Practice Reading Comprehension passages with a timer.

---

### Coding

- Use meaningful variable names.
- Avoid unnecessary nested loops.
- Consider edge cases early.
- Test your solution with small examples.

---

# ❌ Common Mistakes

- Spending too much time on the first question.
- Ignoring easier questions.
- Panicking after a difficult question.
- Reading questions too quickly.
- Not checking sample inputs.
- Leaving coding until the last moment without planning.

---

# 🧘 Staying Calm During the Test

- Read each question carefully.
- Keep an eye on the timer without obsessing over it.
- If stuck, move forward confidently.
- Trust your preparation.
- Avoid comparing yourself to others.

---

# 📅 Practice Like the Real Exam

Before the actual assessment:

- Take at least **10 full-length mock tests**.
- Use a **120-minute timer**.
- Avoid interruptions.
- Review your mistakes after every mock test.
- Track which section consumes the most time and improve it.

---

# ✅ Quick Checklist Before Starting

- ☐ Stable internet connection
- ☐ Fully charged laptop (or power connected)
- ☐ Quiet environment
- ☐ Water bottle nearby
- ☐ Rough sheets and pen (if permitted)
- ☐ Close unnecessary applications
- ☐ Read all on-screen instructions carefully

---

# 💡 Pro Tip

> **The fastest candidate doesn't always get selected. The candidate who manages time wisely, stays calm, and maintains good accuracy has a much better chance of performing well.**
