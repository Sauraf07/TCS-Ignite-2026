# TCS Ignite — 30-Day Coding Prep Roadmap

> Exam: Aug 26, 2026. Today: Jul 20, 2026 → this roadmap finishes by Day 30, leaving buffer days before the exam for revision/rest.
> Daily time assumed: ~1.5–2 hrs/day (adjust up if you have more time on weekends). Use your existing 100-question DSA bank as the practice source — day labels below tell you which tag (Basic/Moderate/Advanced) and topic to pull from.

---

## Week 1 — Python Fluency + Complexity + Arrays/Strings

| Day | Focus | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Python syntax refresh | Loops, functions, list/dict/set operations — W3Schools quick pass, don't over-read |
| 2 | Time & space complexity | Big-O basics (GfG article or freeCodeCamp video) — just enough to judge "fast enough or not" |
| 3 | Arrays — Basic | Solve 8–10 Basic-tagged Array questions from your bank |
| 4 | Arrays — Moderate | Solve 6–8 Moderate-tagged Array questions |
| 5 | Strings — Basic | Solve 8–10 Basic-tagged String questions |
| 6 | Strings — Moderate | Solve 6–8 Moderate-tagged String questions |
| 7 | Review + light day | Redo 3–4 questions you got wrong this week; rest a bit |

### Notes
- If a day's topic feels shaky, it's fine to spend one extra day on it and compress a lighter day (e.g., Day 7 or Day 14) instead — don't skip backtracking or recursion, those are your current weak spot based on the balls-arrangement question.
- Keep a running "mistakes log" (topic + why you got it wrong) from Day 1 — Days 28 and 30 depend on it.
- Verbal Ability (Sentence Completion, Passage Recall, Email Writing) and Aptitude prep run in parallel on your own separate schedule — this roadmap only covers the coding/programming section.
